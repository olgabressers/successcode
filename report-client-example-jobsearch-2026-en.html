The headline

In 2026 work comes from your own applications, not from people you know. This year your CV gets read carefully and answered — even when nobody at the company knows you.

Where to apply. The most willing answers come from companies abroad and from those hiring remotely. They read a CV more carefully and get in touch more often. So do not narrow the search to your own city — look wider.

When to apply. Send applications actively from mid-August. Sign the contract in November: 7 November to 5 December is the best time of the year, when it actually reaches signature.

About October. Something really will shift in October, but unevenly: it is easy to agree to the wrong thing and regret it later. October is for negotiating — sign in November.

✓   Do in 2026
  • ●Send your CV yourself and send many — with specifics: what you did and with what result
  • ●Send the most in late August and September, when you are read best of all
  • ●Look at jobs abroad and remote ones — they answer more often
  • ●Have the contract ready to sign in November (7 November – 5 December)
  • ●Put numbers and scale in the CV, not a list of duties
  • ●Get terms in writing, and always discuss the money
✗   Avoid in 2026
  • ●Do not count on friends bringing you work — it will not come that way this year
  • ●Do not rush to sign in October — the ground is still unsteady
  • ●Do not limit the search to one city — the answers from abroad are better
  • ●Do not accept the first offer — in November you are in a strong position
  • ●A quiet month is not a failure: the good moments come in pinpoints
  • ●Do not send two CVs a month — the year answers both quantity and quality

Important about October. In October (9 October – 6 November) something really will start moving in your work. But the time is unsteady: it is easy to agree to the wrong thing and lose your footing. So in October, talk and negotiate — and sign in November, when it all falls in your favour.

What turns an answer into an offer
Timing and place
Send applications at the end of summer, look abroad and remote, sign in November. October is for talking, not signing. It is not about getting lucky with a vacancy — it is about sending many and signing at the right moment.

Directly, not through people you know. In 2026 work comes like this: you send the CV yourself — many of them, and to the right places. It gets read and answered. Friends will help less this year, so do not spend your main energy there.

The answers from abroad are better. On remote and foreign vacancies your CV is read more carefully and they get in touch more often. Look wider than your own city.

Timing is half the job. Send applications actively from mid-August, sign in November (7 November – 5 December). October is for negotiating and preparing. Actually starting the new job will most likely happen after the beginning of 2027.
