July 2026
The headline

In 2026 work arrives through direct applications with a strong profile — not through contacts. This year your profile reads as authority, and it is the profile that gets answered — even when you apply cold.

Format decides. International and remote roles respond more willingly and read your profile more accurately. Volume, direct applications with a strong CV — that is your channel this year, not "a job through people you know".

Timing decides. Apply actively from mid-August. Sign in November: from 7 November to 5 December the strongest window of the year opens, when an offer reaches signature.

October is a particular month. There genuinely is movement in it, but uneven: in October it is easy both to sign the wrong thing and to lose your footing. October is for negotiation and preparation — the signature belongs to November.

✓   Do in 2026
  • ●Apply in volume and directly — with a strong, precise CV built on results and expertise
  • ●Apply most actively late August – September, when the profile sounds strongest
  • ●Aim at international and remote roles — the response is better there
  • ●Prepare the offer for signature in November (7 November – 5 December)
  • ●Keep the profile "on authority": numbers, scale, results
  • ●Put terms in writing and negotiate on compensation
✗   Avoid in 2026
  • ●Do not bet on "a job through people you know" — this year that is not your channel
  • ●Do not force a signature in October — the window is still uneven
  • ●Do not narrow the search to one geography — international answers better
  • ●Do not accept the first terms — in November your position is strong
  • ●Do not read a quiet month as failure — the windows are pinpoint, not constant
  • ●Do not apply half-heartedly: the year answers volume and the strength of the profile
◆

An important note about October. October (9 October – 6 November) is a month of tangible movement: something in your work situation shifts. But that movement is uneven — in October it is easy to sign the wrong thing and to lose your footing. So October is the time for negotiation and preparation, and the signature on an offer belongs to November, when the window levels out in your favour.

Timing and format
Apply actively at the end of summer, aim at international and remote, press the signature through in November. October is negotiation, not signature. This is not "getting lucky with a vacancy" — it is "applying in volume and signing in the strong window".

Directly, not through contacts. In 2026 the work channel is volume, direct applications with a strong CV. The profile projects authority, and that is what gets answered. "A job through people you know" is weaker this year — do not spend your main energy on it.

International answers better. Remote and foreign roles read your profile more accurately and respond more willingly. Widen the geography, do not narrow it.

Timing is half the result. Apply actively from mid-August; sign in November (7 November – 5 December). October is negotiation and preparation, not signature. The start of a new role is most likely already after the beginning of 2027.
